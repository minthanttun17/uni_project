    Procedure

    1. Taking Data

      It is important that the mortor produces the same performance characterist-ics(eg.horsepower or kilowatt rating and speed) as prior to rewind and that the energy effiency ating is maintained. Moreover, it si also important to note that some of the critical data can't be detetmined later in the winding process.

    2. Coil Cutoff

      It is important to be able to cut off coil extenisons without injury to the stator. The idea is to cut the copper wire, not any other metal part of the stator. Proper cutting of the coil extensions also reduces time and effort when pulling the coils out.

    3. Winding Stripping

      We used winding removal methods and equipments to remove the old winding safely and avoiding damage to the stator core. Closely associated with Winding Stripping is Taking Data, as much of the imortant data is obtained during the stripping process such as connection, turns, spans, poles and grouping.

    4. Coe Perparation Key objectives in Core Preparation are ow to clean, inspect and prepare the bare core and how to repair lamination damage and defects. This step assures that the core is in satisfactory condition prior to actual rewindings and is associated with the Core Testng lesson. The prepared core is core tested to verify that core losses have no increased from the initial ocre test by an unacceptable amount.
    5. Coil Making This is creating new coil from new magnet wire and othe r material. The primary objectives of coil making are recognizing the materials, tools and equipment needed and how to make random wound coils to be installed into a three-phase stator core.
    6. Winding Insulation and Coil Insertion Having made new coils we needed to be inserted into the core bringing up the next step Winding Insulation and Coil Insertion. This is about to determine the material and tools needed and how to instal random wound coils into a three-phase stator core.
    7. Internal Connection

      Even through the winding coils had been made and inserted correctly, the wining would not perform properly if it not connected correctly. This is accomplished in the next step, Internal Connections. From this step I could learn how to identify and lay out winding connectons and the precedures for actually connection the winding.

    8. Varnishing

      The inserted winding coils should be sung in the sung in the slots, but will need reinforcement to reduce the tendency to move or shift. Varnish treatment and curing will do much to make the winding mroe rigid; however there is another key step to be used to reinforce winding coils. That is Lacing and Bracing of Winding, with the main objectives being to make the winder proficient in the methods, materials and procedures for lacing bracing of windings

    9. Testing We tested insulations with testers so that we could know if they were suitable resistance or not.
